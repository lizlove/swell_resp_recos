Takeaways

Our advice on what to spend

You will definitely want to buy a crib that is brand new. It is the only way to make sure that your crib is up to all of the most current safety standards.

What to consider

  • It may seem like a good idea to get a Moses basket or co-sleeper, but there currently are no federally regulated standards for safety with these items.
  • Simple is better. Little details or engravings in the slats of a crib allow an opportunity for your baby's clothing to get caught on them. Simple, straight slats are the safest.
  • Make sure the spaces between the slats are no greater than 2 ⅜ inches wide.
  • Buy new. This is the only way to ensure your crib meets current safety standards and is not already suffering from wear and tear. It is also recommended to buy all crib related items brand new, this will ensure that there is no previous wear and tear and will give you peace of mind while your little one is sleeping.
  • The same goes for your sheets. Make sure the elastic on the sheets is strong and fits extremely tight around the mattress.
