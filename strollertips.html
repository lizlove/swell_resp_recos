Takeaways

Our advice on what to spend

Strollers are one of those items that you are going to be using almost every day. Therefore, we recommend that you pay a few extra bucks for a stroller that is more durable and has more features that will make you and your baby more comfortable.

What to consider

  • Maneuverability is key, it is recommended to test drive your stroller with a 25 pound weight in it to see how it handles with a baby on board.
  • Large wheels are generally more durable and provide a smoother ride than smaller ones.
  • If your stroller does not come with a bassinet or is not a travel system, make sure there is an adapter available that is compatible with your car seat. Your infant will need a few weeks in a bassinet or car seat before they can handle the slight incline that most strollers have.
  • Don’t forget to think about you! Make sure your stroller has plentiful room for all the of the things that you need to take with you. A few strollers can be folded and unfolded with one swift handle pull, this will save you tons of time and effort.
